<div class="study-section-body" style='--x-height-multiplier: 0.375; --baseline-multiplier: 0.17; font-family: medium-content-serif-font, Georgia, Cambria, "Times New Roman", Times, serif; letter-spacing: -.003em; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-size: 21px; line-height: 1.58;'>As with any human experiment, it was impossible to control for all potentially confounding variables. Reproducibility)</b><br>Consistent findings observed by different persons in different places with different samples strengthens the likelihood of an effect. Assuming that the relationship is merely coincidental, as the participant independently modifies their Acne Severity values, the observed strength of the relationship will decline until it is below the threshold of significance.  To it another way, in the case that we do find a spurious correlation, suggesting that banana intake improves mood for instance, one will likely increase their banana intake.  Due to the fact that this correlation is spurious, it is unlikely that you will see a continued and persistent corresponding increase in mood.  So over time, the spurious correlation will naturally dissipate. <br><br><b>Specificity</b><br>Causation is likely if a very specific population at a specific site and disease with no other likely explanation. The more specific an association between a factor and an effect is, the bigger the probability of a causal relationship.<br><br><b>Temporality</b><br>The effect has to occur after the cause (and if there is an expected delay between the cause and expected effect, then the effect must occur after that delay). The confidence in a causal relationship is bolstered by the fact that time-precedence was taken into account in all calculations.<br><br><b>Biological Gradient</b><br>Greater exposure should generally lead to greater incidence of the effect. However, in some cases, the mere presence of the factor can trigger the effect. In other cases, an inverse proportion is observed: greater exposure leads to lower incidence.<br><br><b>Plausibility</b><br>A plausible bio-chemical mechanism between cause and effect is critical.  This is where human brains excel.
